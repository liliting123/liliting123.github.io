Introduction to Jiangmen

Jiangmen, also known as "Wuyi", is a prefecture-level city in Guangdong Province, located in the western part of the Pearl River Delta. It is an important node city in the Guangdong-Hong Kong-Macao Greater Bay Area and a famous hometown of overseas Chinese in China. With a long history and profound cultural heritage, Jiangmen has nurtured many historical celebrities such as Liang Qichao, Chen Baisha, and Feng Ru.

Jiangmen not only has rich natural landscapes but also numerous cultural relics and tourist attractions related to historical celebrities. It is a charming city that combines natural beauty with human history.

Jiangmen Hall of Fame

Liang Qichao (1873-1929)

A modern Chinese thinker, politician, educator, historian, and writer. He was one of the leaders of the Reform Movement of 1898 and a representative figure of modern Chinese reformers and new legalists.

Chen Baisha (1428-1500)

A thinker, educator, calligrapher, and poet of the Ming Dynasty. Born in Xinhui, Jiangmen, he was a pioneer of the Mind Learning school in the Ming Dynasty and was known as "Mr. Baisha".

Feng Ru (1884-1912)

China's first aircraft designer, manufacturer, and aviator, honored as "the Father of Chinese Aviation".

Tang Yin (1470–1524)

  • Renaissance man of the Ming dynasty—painter, poet, calligrapher.
  • Connection with Jiangmen: repeatedly visited Guifeng Mountain in Xinhui; the local legend "Tang Yin Paints Guifeng" is still told today.
  • Representative legacy
    • Painting: Autumn Scenery of Guifeng Mountain
    • Poem: Song of the Peach-Blossom Cottage

"I would rather die drunk among blossoms than kneel before carriages and horses."

Su Shi (1037–1101)

  • Northern Song master of prose, poetry, calligraphy and statesmanship.
  • Connection with Jiangmen: on his way to exile in Hainan (1097) he stayed in the area, climbed Guifeng Mountain, visited Yutai Temple and composed several verses that are still inscribed on local steles.
  • Representative legacy
    • Poem Eating Lychees: "Three hundred lychees a day—gladly would I be a native of Lingnan."
    • Lyric Red Cliff (Nián Nú Jiāo • Chìbì Huáigǔ)

Liang Qichao (1873–1929)

  • Influential modern reformer, political thinker and historian.
  • Connection with Jiangmen: native of Chakeng Village, Xinhui; his former residence is a nationally protected site and a key stop on cultural tours.
  • Representative legacy
    • On Reform (Biànfǎ Tōngyì)
    • Essay Young China: "If the youth are wise, the nation will be wise; if the youth are strong, the nation will be strong."

Celebrity Attractions

Liang Qichao's Former Residence

Located in Chakeng Village, Xinhui District, Jiangmen, it is a national key cultural relics protection unit and the birthplace and growth place of Liang Qichao.

Chen Baisha Memorial Hall

Built to commemorate the Ming Dynasty thinker and educator Chen Baisha, showcasing his life story and academic achievements.

Feng Ru Memorial Museum

A special museum commemorating Feng Ru, the Father of Chinese Aviation, showcasing the early development history of Chinese aviation.

Guifeng Mountain Scenic Area

A famous scenic area in Jiangmen where many literati and scholars left their footprints in history.

Celebrity Stories

Liang Qichao's Educational Philosophy

Liang Qichao attached great importance to education and believed that "education is the foundation of building a nation". He proposed the educational concept of comprehensive development of "moral education, intellectual education, and physical education", emphasizing the cultivation of students' innovative spirit and practical ability. Liang Qichao's educational thoughts have had a profound impact on the development of modern Chinese education.

Feng Ru's Aviation Dream

Feng Ru developed a strong interest in mechanical manufacturing from an early age. To realize China's aviation dream, he traveled across the ocean to the United States to learn advanced technology. In 1909, Feng Ru successfully manufactured and piloted the first aircraft designed by a Chinese person, pioneering the history of Chinese aviation.
